The cliff rule — why it's not a slab

If maintenance charges are ≤ ₹7,500 per member per month, the supply is exempt. Cross ₹7,500 by even ₹1 and 18% GST applies to the ENTIRE amount — not just the excess (CBIC Circular 109/28/2019).

Example — Unit 1101, CAM ₹8,200Taxable
Taxable base
₹8,200 (full amount — not ₹700 excess)
GST @18%
₹1,476 on the full amount
Invoice total
₹9,676

A ₹700 step over the cliff costs the member ₹1,476 — the engine flags cliff-watch units before billing so the committee can decide consciously.

Exempt heads — excluded from the base

Property tax and water at actuals (pure reimbursements collected on behalf of the member) are excluded from the ₹7,500 computation and the GST base. The head-wise split in Collections is what makes this automatic.

⛓ GST lines auto-apply on June invoices in Collections; ₹1.9L flows into the GSTR-1 draft and the Tally export in Finance.

⚖️ Litigation-aware configuration

Madras HC (Greenwood, 2021) quashed the entire-amount basis — held GST applies only to the excess over ₹7,500. The ruling is stayed on appeal; the official CBIC position is applied by default.

If the appeal settles the law, one config change re-bases future invoices — no code, no migration. Every change is audit-logged.
